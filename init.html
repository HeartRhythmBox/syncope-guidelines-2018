Practical Instructions for the 2018 ESC Guidelines for the diagnosis and management of syncope
The complete initial evalution of syncope includes...
H
  history  
  • Orthostatic BP progressive and sustained fall in systolic BP from baseline value ≥20 mmHg or diastolic BP ≥10 mmHg, or a decrease in systolic BP to <90 mmHg.
  • Physical exam
  • ECG
Red
  Flag

Event Detail
  • Syncope during exertion or while supine
  • New chest discomfort, SOB, abd pain, or headache
  • Sudden onset of palpitation followed by syncope

    Past History     		Severe structural heart disease (Low LVEF, CHF, or Old MI)

    Physical Exam
  • Unexplained hypotension
  • Persistent bradycardia; HR<40, in awake state and in the absence of physical training
  • Undiagnosed systolic murmur
  • GI bleeding

    ECG
  • Acute ischemia
  • High grade AV block
  • Sinus pauses or Sinoatrial exit block
  • BBB
  • Old MI
  • Prolong QT
  • Brugada pattern
  • VT or NSVT
  • Dysfunctional of cardiac rhythm device
