  • Symptomatic sinus node dysfunction (I)

  • High grade AV block(I)
  • Bifascicular block and +EPS for HV>70 or inducible AV block (I)

ICD for syncope with...
  • VT and LVEF ≤35% (I)
  • Prior MI and +EPS for VT (I)
  • High-risks HCM (I)
  • LQTs with adequate beta blockers (IIa)
  • Spontaneous type I Brugada (IIa)

  • Recurrent Syncope depends largely on MECHANISM rather than ETIOLOGY.
  • Pacemaker is the most effective therapy for syncope if the main mechanism is bradycardia.
  • Syncopal recurrences often decrease spontaneously after medical assessment, even in the absence of a specific therapy; in general, syncope recurs in <50% of patients within 1â€“2 years
  • Poor outcomes, including death, are related to the severity of the underlying disease rather than to syncope itself.
Driving Restriction
full details
×
  • The risk of syncope related car accident is less than 1%/year.
  • Generally, driving is allowed after "successful therapy is established" in most cases.
  • For single or mild reflex syncope, NO driving restriction unless syncope occurred during driving.
  • In a patient with syncope from cardiac arrhythmia or a patient who had ICD implanted, professinal driving is permanently restricted.
